MAJOR SOMNATH SHARMA, PVC

4th Battalion, Kumaon Regiment

Awards & Honours

  • Param Vir Chakra: India's highest military decoration.
  • Mention-in-Despatches: For bravery during the Arakan Campaign in WWII.

The Legend's Life

Major Somnath Sharma was born into a military family. His father was Major General Amar Nath Sharma. Military service was in his blood. He was commissioned into the 8th Battalion, 19th Hyderabad Regiment in 1942.

The Battle of Badgam

On November 3, 1947, Major Sharma's company was under fire from 700 raiders. Despite being outnumbered 7 to 1, he didn't retreat. Even with a fractured arm in a plaster cast, he was out in the open, helping his men load ammunition.

"The enemy is only 50 yards from us. We are heavily outnumbered. I shall not withdraw an inch but will fight to our last man and our last round."

His sacrifice delayed the enemy long enough for reinforcements to arrive, saving the Srinagar Airport and ultimately, the valley of Kashmir.

The valley of Badgam still echoes with the war cry of the Kumaon Regiment: "Kalika Mata Ki Jai!"
