The Legend's Life

Arun Khetarpal was born into a family of warriors. His father, Brigadier M.L. Khetarpal, was a military engineer. Arun joined the National Defence Academy (NDA) and was later commissioned into the prestigious 17 Poona Horse.

The Battle of Basantar

On December 16, 1971, the Pakistani Army launched a massive tank counter-attack. Arun's troop was outnumbered, but he charged forward. He destroyed 10 enemy tanks personally. Even when his tank was hit and caught fire, his superior officer ordered him to abandon it.

"No, Sir, I will not abandon my tank. My gun is still working and I will get these bastards."

He continued to fire and destroyed the last enemy tank just 100 meters away before his own tank finally succumbed. He saved the Punjab sector from being cut off.

"THE TANK COMMANDER WHO NEVER RETREATED"
